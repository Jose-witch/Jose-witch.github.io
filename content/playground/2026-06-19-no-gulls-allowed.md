Found this one on the way home and laughed out loud on an empty street. A gull, perched on the exact sign that forbids it — the only creature in the city that has never once read the rules.

![A seagull standing on a "no seagulls" sign at dusk](/bird.jpg "the rules, gently ignored")

There's something I love about it. The sign is faded, the bolts are rusting, the painted bird underneath has a red line through its whole life — and above it, alive and unbothered, the real thing. Dusk behind, a soft pink sky, the forest going dark.

![A piebald pony in a wooded paddock, black-and-white film](/intro.jpeg "the other rule-breaker, a few steps on")

A little further on, this one — head down, not a care for fences either. Two small mutinies in one evening walk.

I think about this more than I should. We spend so much effort drawing the line, and the world just sits down on top of it.
